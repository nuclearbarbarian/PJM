About this map ▸

PJM Interconnection is the regional transmission organization coordinating the electric grid across 13 states and D.C., serving 65 million people from Illinois to New Jersey.

This dashboard tracks PJM's power system transformation from 2020 to 2026: the interconnection queue explosion, capacity market stress, data center demand growth, shifting resource mix, and regional import/export dynamics.

Data sources: HIFLD (plants, transmission, boundary), EIA Forms 923 & 930 (generation, interchange), PJM public queue exports (interconnection queue), PJM RTEP project list (cross-reference for planned routes), NERC LTRA (reserve margins), PJM RPM BRA reports (capacity prices), IM3 Open Source Data Center Atlas/PNNL (data centers, DOI: 10.57931/2550666, ~80% coverage pre-2024), Piedmont Environmental Council/ArcGIS Online (planned RTEP route geometries, NIETC corridor boundary).

Known limitations: Plant locations from HIFLD may lag 6–12 months. Queue data shown in charts/KPIs only — not mapped due to geocoding imprecision (county centroids fall outside PJM zones in partial-coverage states). Generation totals use full-state EIA data, which overcounts for states only partially in PJM. Data centers from IM3/PNNL atlas (DOI: 10.57931/2550666) — peer-reviewed post-processing of OSM, ~80% complete for pre-2024 major facilities (CoreWeave, Yondr absent). Includes sqft for building-layer facilities but no MW power data. RTEP routes are PEC digitizations from developer filings — 7 of 25 proposals confirmed against PJM's official RTEP project list; PJM does not publish route GIS (CEII). NIETC corridor is PEC's interpretation of DOE preliminary maps — DOE has not published official GIS for the Mid-Atlantic corridor. Reserve margin projections read from NERC charts, not official tables.

Flags: [VERIFY] = third-party digitization, cross-checked where possible. [ESTIMATE] = projected or annualized. [GAP] = known incomplete source with coverage estimate.
